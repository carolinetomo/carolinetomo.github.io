About me


I am a computational evolutionary paleobiologist and vertebrate paleontologist. My research is focused on identifying the patterns and higher-level processes underlying the emergence of novel suites of phenotypic traits. This involves incorporating organismal complexity from the developmental to the ecological levels better understand both the external contexts and underlying constraints that guide phenotypic innovation. My empirical research interests often require methodological efforts to build an analytical framework that can effectively leverage emerging sources of large-scale morphological data to reconstruct evolutionary relationships and broad-scale patterns among fossil taxa.
